Kendall's Bake Ziti Pasta

Description

A delicious baked ziti with italian sausage, and multiple cheeses.
This is my mom's recipe!
Prep Time: 15 minutes | Cook Time: | 30 minutes | Total Time: | 45 minutes

Ingredients

  • 1 lb. Ziti Pasta
  • 1-2 Jars of Rao's Marinara Sauce
  • 2 Cup Kraft Italian Five Cheese Blend

    For Alfredo Sauce

  • 3 tbsp butter
  • 3 tbsp flour
  • 1 1/2 tsp minced garlic
  • 2 cups milk
  • 1 cup Kraft Parmesan Cheese
  • 1 1/2 tsp garlic powder *Or to taste*
  • Salt and pepper to taste

      Intructions

    1. Preheat your oven to 375 degrees. Grease a 9 x 13 baking dish.
    2. Cook your pasta according to the package but leave it a little under-done, so it is not too soft. It will soften and cook a bit more in the oven. When the pasta is cooked just under-done, drain it, and rinse it in cold water so it stops cooking. Set it aside.

      For Alfredo Sauce

    1. Melt your butter in a skillet over medium heat and add your flour and minced garlic. Whisk it together and let it cook for 1-2 minutes until the garlic becomes fragrant and butter and flour start to bubble.
    2. Add your milk to the butter and flour and whisk it together until mixed well. Continue to whisk the sauce over medium heat until it starts to thicken.
    3. Add your cream cheese and whisk until the cream cheese is melted and sauce is smooth.

      Making the Pasta Dish

    1. Mix the Alfredo and Marinara Sauce together. You can adjust the seasonings at this point as well if needed. You can add more garlic powder, salt etc.
    2. Mix in the cooked pasta. Mix well.
    3. Take half of your grated mozzarella cheese (about 1 cup) and mix in the pasta and sauce.*If you are adding cooked meat or veggies in the dish, add it now!
    4. Pour the pasta in a greased 9×13 baking dish. Cover with foil.
    5. Bake at 375° for 30 minutes. Take the foil off the last 10

    Kwame's Ghanaian Meat Pies

    Description

    Flaky pastry filled with minced beef, onions and green peppers.
    These meat pies are delicious and a popular Ghanaian snack.
    Servings: 8 | Prep Time: 1 hour | Cook Time: 30 minutes | Calories: 4127kcal |

    Ingredients

      Meat Pie Filling
    • 1 tablespoon sunflower oil
    • 300 grams, minced beef
    • 1 medium onion, chopped
    • ½ teaspoon, all purpose seasoning
    • 1 small maggi stock cube
    • Salt to taste
    • 1 large green pepper, chopped into small cubes
      Pastry
    • 400 grams (3⅓ cups) self raising flour
    • 255 grams (1 cup plus 1 tablespoon) cold margarine or butter
    • Pinch of salt
    • 60 ml (¼ cup) cold water
    • 1 egg, whisked

    Instructions

      Minced Beef Filling
    1. Add a little oil to a large frying pan and heat up. Add the minced beef and onions and cook
      on medium heat until it browns.
    2. Mix in tomato puree and cook for 2 minutes. Add in all purpose seasoning stock cube and salt.
      Remove from heat and leave to cool, then stir in chopped green peppers.
      Pastry
    1. Sift flour and salt into a large mixing bowl.
    2. Add margarine to the flour and rub in using your fingertips. Add the cold water and mix.
    3. Turn the dough onto a lightly floured work surface and knead very lightly.
    4. Shape the dough into a disk and wrap tightly in plastic wrap and refrigerate until well chilled, at least an hour or overnight.
      To Assemble
    1. Pre-heat oven to 180°C fan/200°/400°F.
    2. Line a large baking tray with greaseproof paper.
    3. Lightly dusk a work surface and a rolling pin with flour. Roll out chilled dough.
      (This should be carried out in short, sharp stroked with light even pressure in a forward movement only. Turn the pastry as you roll.)
    4. Using either a saucer or a large circle cutter, cut circles in the dough.
      Place a quarter cup of filling in the centre of the circle.
    5. Fold dough over making it into a semicircle. Take a pastry brush and dip in water and moisten edges of dough circle then pinch sides together with a fork.
      Use a fork and poke holes on the top of the meat pies.
    6. Place meat pies on the baking tray.
    7. Brush the tops of pies with egg wash and bake in oven for 25 minutes or until the pies are golden brown.

    Ken's Baked Steak & Potatoes

    Description

    This protein-packed meal is simple, quick, and affordable! Contents contain steak, potatoes, and shredded cheese guaranteed to be flavorful and fulfilling!
    Highly recommended for large-size families who need something quick to whip up. Also personally recommended for college students on a budget and those who aren't too fond of the kitchen.
    Yield: 4 Servings | Prep Time: 10 minutes | Cook Time: 50 minutes

    Ingredients

    • 1 pound of Beef Stew Meat
    • 1 pound of Baby yellow potatoes
    • 8 ounce bag of shredded Mexican Cheese
    • 1/2 teaspoon of tony chacheres and Black Pepper
    • 3 tablespoons of butter
    • 1 teaspoon of parsley

    Intructions

    1. Preheat oven to 400 degrees Fahrenheit
    2. In a large pot bring potatoes to a rolling boil for approximately 15 to 20 minutes. While the potatoes are boiling, season beef stew meat with tony chacheres, black pepper, and any other seasonings of your liking.
    3. Remove potatoes from large pot and cut into small cubes. Then combine potatoes,beef stew meat, and butter into a deep dish baking tray.
    4. Cover baking tray with aluminum foil. Place baking tray and its contents into preheated oven for 40 minutes.
    5. Once the 40 minutes have passed, remove baking tray from oven and pull the foil back. Apply shredded cheese on top of the entire dish and reapply the aluminum foil.
    6. Place baking tray back into the oven and let cook for another 10 minutes.
    7. Remove from oven, garnish with parsley, and serve!
